CRITICAL EVALUATION MINDSET
–––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Before scoring, ask yourself these REALITY CHECK questions:

1. **The 3-Second Test**: If YOU were walking past this window while checking your phone, would THIS banner make you STOP and look up?
   - If answer is "maybe" or "probably not" → Strong indicator to FAIL

2. **The Desire Test**: Does this banner make you genuinely WANT the product/service?
   - For food: Does it make your mouth water?
   - For deals: Does it create urgency/FOMO?
   - If answer is "not really" → Strong indicator to FAIL

3. **The Professional Standard Test**: Could this banner appear in a successful chain restaurant or retail store?
   - Compare mentally to Starbucks, McDonald's, Target-level quality
   - If it feels "small business amateur" → Strong indicator to FAIL

4. **The Competition Test**: If a competitor's banner was next to this one, would this one WIN attention?
   - If answer is "no" or "they're equal" → Strong indicator to FAIL

If a banner fails ANY of these reality checks, look extra carefully at the checkpoints.

CHECKPOINTS (ALL MUST PASS)
–––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

### 1. attention (CRITICAL - BE STRICT)
**Question**: Does the banner immediately grab attention and have a strong visual focal point?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- There is ONE clear, dominant focal point (headline, hero image, or price)
- Visual hierarchy is obvious and intentional (your eye knows where to go first)
- The design uses bold colors, high contrast, or striking imagery that commands attention
- Important elements are sized prominently (headline at least 20% of banner height, prices are oversized)
- The banner would be noticed by someone walking past at normal speed (3-5 seconds of visibility)

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- No clear focal point, eye doesn't know where to look first
- Multiple elements compete equally for attention
- Design is visually bland, muted, or dull (wouldn't stand out in a busy environment)
- Headline/key elements are too small relative to banner size
- Layout feels cluttered or unbalanced
- Colors are boring/monotone/low-energy
- Design looks like it was made in Microsoft Word or basic Canva template

**STRICT STANDARD**: Ask yourself: "Would I personally notice this banner in my peripheral vision while walking past?" If no → FAIL

---

### 2. clarity (CRITICAL - BE STRICT)
**Question**: Can the main message be understood in under 2 seconds?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- Main offer/message is instantly obvious and front-and-center
- Message is concise and benefit-focused (not wordy or vague)
- Text hierarchy makes the most important info (offer, product, price) pop out
- There is NOT too much text competing for attention
- A distracted viewer could understand the offer in 1-2 seconds

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- Message takes 3+ seconds to figure out
- Headline is generic, weak, or buried in other text
- Too much text dilutes the core message
- Multiple messages compete (confusing what the banner is really about)
- Offer/benefit is unclear (e.g., "Special Deal" without specifics)
- Important details (price, timing) are hard to find

**STRICT STANDARD**: Cover the banner, then uncover for 2 seconds. Could you explain the offer? If no → FAIL

---

### 3. emotion (CRITICAL - BE VERY STRICT)
**Question**: Does the design trigger genuine desire, appetite, urgency, or positive emotion?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- Design creates a visceral emotional response (hunger for food, excitement for deals, trust for services)
- For FOOD: Images look professionally shot, fresh, hot, appetizing, make you crave it
- For OFFERS: Creates urgency, FOMO, or excitement (not just "here's info")
- For SERVICES: Builds trust, professionalism, or aspirational feeling
- Uses emotional triggers: scarcity ("Limited Time"), social proof, aspirational imagery
- Color psychology is used effectively (warm colors for food, urgent colors for deals)

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- Design is purely informational with NO emotional hook
- Food imagery looks cold, stale, unappetizing, or generic stock photo
- No sense of urgency, desire, or FOMO created
- Messaging is passive (e.g., "Available Now" vs "Get Yours Today!")
- Viewer feels neutral or indifferent after seeing it
- Missing emotional language (e.g., "Fresh," "Hot," "Limited," "New," "Exclusive")

**STRICT STANDARD**: After viewing, do you feel hunger/desire/urgency? If your honest answer is "not really" → FAIL

---

### 4. typography (STRICT EVALUATION)
**Question**: Is the text professionally executed with excellent readability?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- Fonts are professional, modern, and highly legible (not decorative fonts for body text)
- Clear font hierarchy: headline is BOLD and large, subtext is clearly smaller
- Maximum 2-3 font families used (not a chaotic mix)
- Text size is appropriate for viewing distance (headline dominates, prices are large)
- Line spacing and letter spacing are comfortable (not cramped or overlapping)
- Text alignment is clean and intentional
- Font choices match the brand tone (playful, premium, casual, etc.)

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- Fonts look amateur, dated, or hard to read (Comic Sans, over-decorative fonts)
- Headline is too small (less than 15-20% of banner height)
- Poor font hierarchy (everything is similar size, hard to scan)
- 4+ different font styles used (looks chaotic)
- Text is cramped, overlapping, or has poor spacing
- Font choices clash with each other or brand tone
- Important text (prices, key terms) is not prominent enough

**STRICT STANDARD**: Could you read the headline from 15 feet away? If no → FAIL

---

### 5. contrast (STRICT EVALUATION)
**Question**: Do all important elements have excellent contrast and visual separation?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- Text has strong contrast against background (minimum 4.5:1 ratio, ideally 7:1+)
- Headlines and prices are easily readable with no eye strain
- No important text is placed on busy backgrounds without proper text backing/shadow
- Color combinations create visual energy and pop
- Key elements stand out clearly from their surroundings
- Strategic use of accent colors to highlight important info

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- ANY important text is hard to read due to low contrast
- Text placed directly on busy images/patterns without backing
- Color scheme is muddy, dull, or washed out
- Background and foreground colors are too similar
- Viewer has to squint or strain to read anything important
- Design feels flat or lacks visual punch due to poor contrast

**STRICT STANDARD**: Is there even ONE piece of important text that's slightly hard to read? If yes → FAIL

---

### 6. imagery (CRITICAL - BE VERY STRICT)
**Question**: Are all images professional-quality, high-resolution, and emotionally effective?

**PASS criteria (ALL must be true):**
- All product/food images are PROFESSIONALLY photographed (not phone snapshots)
- Images are sharp, high-resolution, and properly lit
- For FOOD: Looks fresh, hot, appetizing, professionally styled (shows texture, steam, garnish)
- For PRODUCTS: Well-composed, shows key details, aspirational
- Image composition is intentional (proper framing, rule of thirds, focus)
- Images directly support the message (no random stock photos)
- Lighting is professional (soft, even, shows true colors)

**FAIL if ANY of these:**
- Images are blurry, pixelated, or low-resolution (even if from the printed banner itself)
- Food looks cold, stale, unappetizing, or poorly plated
- Images are obviously cheap stock photos or clip art
- Lighting is harsh, dark, or makes products look unappealing
- Image composition is amateur (bad framing, awkward cropping, distracting elements)
- Missing hero image when one is essential (e.g., food offer with no food photo)
- Images don't match the offer (misleading or unrelated visuals)

**STRICT STANDARD**: Would this imagery appear in a chain restaurant or professional ad campaign? If no → FAIL

ADDITIONAL STRICT REQUIREMENTS

Beyond the 6 checkpoints, also check these DEAL-BREAKERS:

**Automatic FAIL conditions (override everything else):**

4. **Confusing layout**: Multiple competing messages, no clear CTA, viewer doesn't know what action to take

If ANY automatic fail condition exists, the banner FAILS regardless of checkpoint scores.
